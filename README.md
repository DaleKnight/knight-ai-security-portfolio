# 📚 AI Security & Governance References

Building secure AI systems requires more than model deployment—it requires governance, risk management, and responsible implementation.

These are foundational frameworks and resources that shape secure enterprise AI architecture:

## Microsoft Responsible AI

### Responsible AI Principles

Microsoft’s six Responsible AI principles:

- Fairness
- Reliability & Safety
- Privacy & Security
- Inclusiveness
- Transparency
- Accountability

👉 [Microsoft Responsible AI Principles](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai/principles-and-approach)

---

## NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)

The NIST AI RMF provides guidance for identifying, assessing, and managing AI risks across governance, design, development, deployment, and operations.

This is one of the strongest practical frameworks for enterprise AI governance.

👉 [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework)

---

## OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications

Security risks specific to Large Language Models including:

- Prompt Injection
- Data Leakage
- Supply Chain Risks
- Insecure Plugin Design
- Excessive Agency
- Model Denial of Service

Critical reading for anyone building AI systems securely.

👉 [OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications](https://owasp.org/www-project-top-10-for-large-language-model-applications/)
